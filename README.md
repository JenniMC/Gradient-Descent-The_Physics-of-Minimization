# Gradient-Descent-The_Physics-of-Minimization
Have you ever wondered how AI models improve their predictions? The short answer is: Gradient Descent.  To understand it, imagine a metal ball rolling down from the top of a mountain. Its goal is to reach the lowest point (the valley) using gravity. In Data Science, we do the same thing to optimize our algorithms.

Here are the key concepts behind this process:

🔹 The Cost Function: This is our "landscape." It represents the error we want to minimize.

🔹 The Gradient: This is the slope of the terrain. It tells us which direction to move to go down faster.

🔹 Learning Rate ($\alpha$): This is our step size. If the rate is too high, we might miss the valley; if it is too low, it will take too long to arrive.

Optimization is not just math; it is about finding the perfect balance between speed and accuracy.
